Blood donation is a simple and safe process that can save lives. The process typically begins with a quick registration and medical screening to ensure the donor is in good health. Next, the donor will have their vital signs checked, such as blood pressure and hemoglobin levels. The actual donation process involves the use of a sterile needle to draw blood from a vein in the arm. The entire process usually takes about an hour and the donor can return to normal activities immediately after. Donors are encouraged to drink fluids and eat a healthy meal to maintain their strength after the donation. The donated blood is then tested for various diseases and sent to hospitals for use in medical treatments. With each donation, a single person can help save multiple lives.
